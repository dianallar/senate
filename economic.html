Economic Policy

An economy that works from the middle, out—not the top down.

Cutting Costs for Working Families by Curbing Excessive Spending and Greed.

We'll fight inflation at its root—corporate profiteering and supply chain failures—using every tool of government to lower prices while protecting workers and families. The fact of the matter is this: The government keeps spending money it does not have, weakening the purchasing power of the US Dollar. Year on Year inflation is approaching 10% (graph 2) and it's actively harming the American consumer. As your Senator, I will work to combat inflation through the following measures:

1. Windfall Profit Redistribution

  • 50% Excess Profits Tax on corporations in healthcare, energy, and housing when margins exceed 150% of historic averages
  • Revenue Directly Funds:
    • Public Option premium subsidies
    • Construction of 500,000 affordable housing units annually
    • Monthly $200 "Price Relief Cards" for essentials

2. Strategic Supply Buffers

  • National Stockpiles of:
    • Generic drugs (leveraging our public pharmaceutical factories)
    • Building materials (lumber, cement, steel)
    • Staple foods (formula, grains, cooking oil)
  • Automatic Releases when prices spike >10% above normal

3. Anti-Gouging Enforcement

  • Corporate Price Audits: FTC subpoena power for corporate books
  • 500% Penalties on illicit price hikes (matching our healthcare law sanctions)
  • Mandatory Rollbacks for unjustified increases in healthcare/utilities

4. Public Competition

  • Medicare Manufacturing of 200+ generic drugs (already authorized)
  • FedRent Program offering lease-to-own homes at 30% below market
  • Postal Banking with 0% emergency loans when inflation exceeds 5%

5. Automatic Stabilizers

  • Sales Tax Holidays triggered when inflation exceeds 4% for 3 months
  • Corporate Rebate Clawbacks for federal contractors who raise prices faster than wages

"Inflation is a policy choice—we choose to side with workers over Wall Street."

“When workers thrive, the economy thrives.”

Real wage gains shouldn't fuel price spirals. We'll rebalance power toward workers through structural reforms that increase pay sustainably. I have routinely fought for sustainable wage increases, namely when I increased the minimum wage for educators in the state of Olympia and ensured that teaching could remain an equitable profession, creating numerous jobs and ending the teacher shortage in Olympia. As your Senator, I will take the following steps to promote worker power and wage growth:

1. Sectoral Bargaining

  • Industry-Wide Wage Boards setting minimum pay/benefits by sector (starting with healthcare, retail, and logistics)
  • Multi-Employer Unions allowing workers at small businesses to bargain collectively
  • Productivity-Linked Raises requiring profit growth to outpace executive pay

2. Public Option for Good Jobs

  • Federal Job Guarantee at $25/hr + benefits in caregiving, infrastructure, and climate resilience
  • Union Hiring Halls prioritizing workers displaced by automation/transition
  • Worker Ownership Fund financing employee buyouts of failing firms

3. Wage-Price Firewall

  • Excess Profit Recapture taxing corporations that raise prices faster than wages
  • CEO Pay Ratio Cap of 50:1 for federal contractors (vs current 320:1 average)
  • Right to Own Your Data guaranteeing workers a share of AI/automation profits

4. Enforcement Revolution

  • Triple NLRB Funding to process unfair labor practice claims in <30 days
  • Corporate Death Penalty revoking charters of repeat wage-theft violators
  • Global Labor Standards banning imports from union-busting firms

"When workers have power, the economy works for everyone—without price spikes."

“Build more, bottleneck less.”

Real price stability comes from increasing supply—not crushing demand. We'll break corporate monopolies, rebuild domestic capacity, and cut dependence on fragile global chains. Here are some things we can do to accomplish that:

1. Public Production for Critical Goods

  • Medicare Manufacturing 2.0:
    • Double generic drug output using Fix The Public Option Act facilities
    • Produce 50+ active pharmaceutical ingredients domestically by 2026
  • National Infrastructure Labs:
    • Develop low-cost modular construction tech
    • Mass-produce climate-resilient building materials

2. Permitting Revolution

  • 90-Day Guarantee for clean energy/transit projects
  • Community Benefit Agreements fast-tracking projects that:
    • Pay union wages
    • Include local hiring
    • Meet climate standards

3. Competitive Markets

  • Break Up "Zombie" Monopolies:
    • Force divestment of idle patents/real estate
    • Ban vertical integration in food/healthcare
  • Open-Source Industrial Policy:
    • Publicly license federally funded R&D
    • Launch 10 "Manufacturing Extension Hubs"

4. Resilient Logistics

  • National Shipping Reform:
    • Price caps on container freight
    • Public port modernization
  • Strategic Farm Capacity:
    • Revive agricultural cooperatives
    • Stockpile key fertilizers/seeds

Shortages shall become obsolete through public investment, not corporate handouts.

“Hold the powerful to account.”

We'll dismantle the corporate price-setting power that drives inflation, with surgical reforms to punish profiteering and rebalance our economy. I have long stood in the way of corporate greed and overreach, through measures that I signed into law as President to ensure market competition and by attempting to dismantle the anonymous organization of Political Action Committees and expose where the money goes and just which big companies are funding elections. As your Senator, I will continue to combat the constant assault on our economy by corporate overlords, via these methods:

1. Profit & Price Controls

  • Windfall Profit Caps:
    • Automatic 75% tax on corporate profits exceeding 10% margin in essential sectors (healthcare, housing, utilities)
    • Revenue funds direct consumer rebates
  • CEO Clawbacks:
    • Mandate return of bonuses when firms raise prices >2x inflation rate
    • Personal liability for wage theft

2. Market Structure Reform

  • Break Up Shadow Cartels:
    • Ban common ownership of competitors (e.g., BlackRock owning rival airlines)
    • Criminal penalties for algorithmic price-fixing
  • Public Competitors:
    • Expand Public Option Act model to housing (FedHomes) and utilities
    • Undercut private sector price gouging

3. Transparency & Enforcement

  • Real-Time Price Audits:
    • Mandate public cost breakdowns for essential goods
    • FTC "SWAT teams" for surprise inspections
  • Whistleblower Armies:
    • 25% bounty on recovered price-fixing fines
    • Protection + relocation for corporate whistleblowers

4. Structural Consequences

  • Three Strikes Law:
    • Revoke corporate charters after repeated violations
    • Convert abandoned assets to worker cooperatives
  • Investor Accountability:
    • Pierce the corporate veil for institutional shareholders
    • Ban stock buybacks for firms raising prices > inflation

If corporations won't serve the public, I'll replace them with institutions that do.

“A fair shot, not a lifetime of debt.”

Crushing household debt fuels inflation by forcing families to borrow more just to survive. As your Senator, I will cancel predatory debts and rebuild economic security through public alternatives. Here is my plan for lowering Pacificans' personal debts:

1. Medical Debt Revolution

  • Total Erasure:
    • Cancel all $220B in outstanding medical debt using Public Option Act authority
    • Ban medical debt collections and credit reporting permanently
  • Prevention:
    • Cap out-of-pocket costs at $500/year under public option
    • Automatic enrollment in zero-interest payment plans

2. Student Debt Overhaul

  • Borrower's Bill of Rights:
    • Limit payments to 5% of disposable income
    • Forgiveness after 10 years of payments (5 years for public service)
  • Public University Compact:
    • Federal takeover of student lending
    • 0% interest for graduates working in high-need fields

3. Housing Debt Justice

  • Foreclosure Moratorium:
    • 5-year pause on evictions for medically/financially distressed households
    • Right-to-rent conversions for foreclosed properties
  • Usury Limits:
    • National 15% interest rate cap
    • Ban junk fees on mortgages/rental applications

4. Public Safety Nets

  • Postal Banking:
    • 3% emergency loans during inflationary spikes
    • Check cashing/payday loan alternatives
  • Debt Strike Insurance:
    • Federal protection for workers withholding payments from fraudulent creditors
    • Class-action leverage against abusive lenders

“No one left behind in a changing economy.”

Rapid economic change can leave too many behind—whether from automation, globalization, or climate transition. As your Senator, I’ll ensure every Pacifican has a bridge to new opportunity, not a trapdoor to poverty. We’ll protect workers, families, and communities through every transition with these safeguards:

1. Guaranteed Retraining & Reemployment

  • Universal Retraining Grants for workers displaced by automation, offshoring, or industry shifts
  • Job Placement Guarantees—no one left without a path to new work
  • Paid Transition Periods with full wage replacement for up to 12 months

2. Community Resilience Funds

  • Targeted Investment in regions hit by plant closures or industry decline
  • Local Economic Diversification grants for new businesses and green jobs
  • Public Works Surge to create jobs rebuilding infrastructure and climate resilience

3. Safety Net Expansion

  • Automatic Unemployment Extensions during mass layoffs or recessions
  • Portable Benefits—healthcare, retirement, and paid leave that follow you to any job
  • Emergency Housing & Food Assistance for families in transition

4. Worker Voice in Transition

  • Worker Transition Councils at every major employer to plan and negotiate fair transitions
  • Advance Notice & Consultation before layoffs, closures, or automation
  • Right to Buy—workers get first chance to purchase and run facilities facing closure

Change is inevitable—but hardship is not. Together, we'll ensure a just transition that uplifts everyone.
